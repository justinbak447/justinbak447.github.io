The Dynamics of a Carving Snowboarder

In this project, I find how to mathematically describe the dynamics of a snowboarder navigating down a mountain, using both the Differential Algebraic Equations (DAE) method and Lagrangian method. At a certain roll angle, the snowboarder experiences "carving" where they are constrained to a carving radius. This behavior is modeled in MATLAB, the code of which is included in the paper's Apppendix.

Comparative Analysis of Propulsion Methods for a Mission to Mars

In this project, my colleagues and I assess the feasibility of Nuclear Thermal Propulsion (NTP) for a planned mission to Mars. To do this, we compare time constraints, performance metrics, and logistical outcomes for the aforementioned method to that of chemical and electric propulsion.

Design and Fabrication of a Turbulent Wind Tunnel

In my senior year at the University of South Carolina, I was tasked with designing a low Reynold's number wind tunnel. My teammates and I performed hand calculations, threee-dimensional CFD simulations, and finite element analysis to ensure the success of the project, which fit under a budget of $1250.
